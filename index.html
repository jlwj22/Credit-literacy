What is a Credit Score?

üí° Credit Score Definition

A Credit Score is a three-digit number (300-850) that represents your creditworthiness. It tells lenders how likely you are to repay borrowed money on time.

üè¢ Created by Fair Isaac Corporation

Credit scores are calculated using various models, with the most common being developed by Fair Isaac Corporation in 1989. These scoring systems help lenders assess risk.

üåü Why It Matters

Your credit score affects:

  • Whether you get approved for loans and credit cards
  • The interest rates you'll pay
  • Credit limits you'll receive
  • Sometimes even job opportunities and housing
